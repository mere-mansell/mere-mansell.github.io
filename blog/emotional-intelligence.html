Emotional Intelligence.

WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN EQ AND IQ?

WHAT IS IQ?

IQ or Intelligence Quotient, is a reflection of someone’s academic ability. Up until the late 90s, IQ was largely thought of as the primary way to determines one’s success. Your IQ determines your competency towards individual tasks.

WHAT IS EQ?

EQ or Emotional Intelligence or the Emotional Quotient, is someone’s ability to identify, evaluate, control and express emotions. Your EQ determines how you interact with and treat the people around you.

WHY IS EQ IMPORTANT?

Someone with high EQ is able able to recognise, control and express one’s own emotion while perceiving and assessing other’s emotions. In comparison, someone with a high IQ can learn, understand and implement knowledge and possesses logical reasoning and abstract thinking.

High EQ is usually a skill needed for and seen in many leaders, team captains, managers and people with social challenges. Any role which requires the management of a team to ensure the team operate most efficiently and effectively will perform better if led by someone with high EQ.
