Sprint 2: Learning Plan.

HOW WILL I OPERATE WITHIN FOUNDATIONS AND BOOTCAMP

WHAT IS YOUR CAREER GOAL?

I’ve never really been someone who can clearly picture, verbalise or identify what I envision for my future. It’s been really good for enabling me to jump at opportunities to learn new skills, but not the best in terms of having a solid aim or something to concretely work towards.

The more I try out new things, the easier it is for me to decide if I enjoy something or not and if I want it to be a part of my future. After working in my last role, I know I would really like a career that offered remote working where I can be a part of a team from the comfort of where ever I am.

I had always had an interest in coding and my unclear career pathway and keen, up for the challenge mentality jumped at the chance to learn web development, acquire a new skill and see if it can provide a career pathway for me.

End of Sprint 5: Honestly not sure yet. I want to work in an environment that aligns with my values and supports my idea of a work/life balance. I would like to work on projects where I feel like I am contributing to something meaningful and impactful. Something to contribute to positive outcomes for Māori would be amazing. Dev, am I pretty much describing a job with you guys? ;)

MY STRENGTHS AND WEAKNESSES

I am detail orientated, logical, energetic, a quick thinker and original. The limitations of these strengths when it comes to learning is that I’m logical to the point where I want to fully understand how something works, and every possible option and outcome before I make a decision. This means I can be slow to make decisions and inflexible if logic says otherwise.

Because I am energetic and a quick thinker, I can find it difficult to focus and can get bored or lose interest easily especially if I’m lacking the inspiration for a project I may have initially had.

End of Sprint 5: My idea around these hasn’t changed much since the beginning of Foundations, however, they may change and develop throughout bootcamp. A limitation that I really need to challenge during Bootcamp is my confidence and being brave enough to try despite whether or not a solution will work. I’m still a bit hesitant with things because I still see myself as a beginner.

WHAT NON-TECH SKILLS DO I WANT TO DEVELOP?

I am brand new to coding and web develop so I came into Dev really focussed on learning the technical side of the programme. However, in my two weeks of foundations so far I have already seen I have a huge amount to gain on the human skills aspect as well.

Time management is something I have always struggled to get right, but with Dev I have noticed that this becomes critical as to not get left behind with the intensive workload.

Learning how to prioritise and balance a heavy workload with outside influences will be interesting too. Just seeing how I will cope mentally and emotionally if things start to get hectic will be a learning process.

End of Sprint 5: There were definitely times during Foundations where I felt tired and run down but was still able to manage the workload. I’m worried about staying on top of the workload in Bootcamp and keeping energised and motivated for 9 weeks. I don’t want to burn out, but the workload is super intensive, so this will be something I need to assess weekly and see how I’m coping. I really want to develop my self-awareness and reflective skills and make it something I do regularly. I’m really going to work on implementing the strategies of core and have a daily practice of self-check in and reflection at the end beginning and end of the day.

HOW WILL I MANAGE MY WORKLOAD?

Setting and getting habitual with a routine is going to be really key to staying on top of things in bootcamp and ensuring that I spend enough time engaging with the workload, sleeping, eating well and exercising.

Even though this programme is super intensive, I really want to make sure I am balancing it with my pillars of health (sleep, eating, connection with others, exercise, contributing to others). I believe if I can dedicate adequate time to things outside of this programme, it will help me perform at my best during bootcamp.

Little things like making a clear time that I will dedicate to code and a clear time that I will shut off for the evening. Having a bedtime and a wake time and setting these intentionally to ensure I can fit in a gym workout or walk and making nutritious food in my day. I want to be committing at minimum 50-60hours a week to code during bootcamp.

I have a study space in my home and my whānau are well aware of the commitments required for this course and are supportive of my space. I also have access to study spaces outside of my place if I need a change of scenery.

Other things I will need to schedule into my days include the nights where I cook dinner, driving to and from co-working space and time to practice my te reo Māori which is a huge priority in my life.

End of Sprint 5: I have set up my work space to ensure that I have somewhere to work productively during bootcamp. I purchased a second screen so that coding and follow-alongs will be easier for me. For the next 9 weeks, things will just need to become routine. My plan is to be up every day at 6am to start the day with a walk, breakfast and some self-reflection and goal setting for the day before class starts at 9am.

During lunch time, it’s really important to me to switch off and focus on something else, and for me that is practicing my te reo Māori. I hope to be finished majority of my daily tasks at 6pm as I would still like to be available to help with dinner if I can. However, I know that this will not be a possibility every night and am okay coding longer as long as I am done and shut off for the night at about 10/10.30pm. Again, this is something that I will need to reassess as we progress through bootcamp. I know the workload is intensive but it’s a bit hard to envision what that will actually look like for 9 weeks. Everyone in my life knows that I am starting bootcamp and everyone is really supportive and understanding going into the next two months that I’ll be super busy and not available as much as I am now during foundations.

It's quite scary and I’m really hoping I’ll be able to keep up with the workload, so putting systems in place is very important for success.

WHEN I NEED HELP

When I run into issues my first point of action is to always see if I can figure it out using google or youtube or searching the discord to see if someone else has had the same issue before.

However, I understand that while this is good, spending too much time researching for a solution can actually prove counter intuitive and so setting a timeframe to allow for research before asking is a good idea. For example, “I’ll try to figure it out for 25minutes, if I haven’t in that time I will ask the facilitation team for guidance."

I expect the facilitation team to point me in the right direction or challenge my thinking to find a possible solution without directly fixing something for me. I also expect the facilitation team to be empathetic and understanding when it comes to timeframes and potential outside impacts out of our control.

End of Sprint 5: Based on bootcamp, I feel more confident reaching out for help if I need assistance quicker. For example, if I am stuck on a problem for more than 30mins, I will reach out for some guidance.

I expect the bootcamp team to be encouraging, supportive, understanding and gentle with me. I don’t expect full answers or hand-holding, but learning code is quite scary for me and I’m definitely feeling outside of my comfort zone. So when learning, I expect gentle responses and aids when I make mistakes. When someone’s learning it’s really easy to be discouraged from wanting to continue learning, so I hope if I do make mistakes people will be gentle and patient with me.
